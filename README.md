<br>
POMODORI is a distraction-free Pomodoro timer built around one idea: helping you focus.<br>
No account required. No syncing to a server. No notifications you did not ask for. Just a timer, your work, and a soft sound when time is up.


&nbsp;
<pre>No signup          Open the page and start working immediately </pre>
<pre>No backend         Runs entirely in your browser, nothing leaves your device </pre>
<pre>No tracking        Zero cookies, zero analytics, zero data collection </pre>
<pre>Three modes        Pomodoro, short break, and long break in one click </pre>
<pre>Session tracking   Four-dot cycle so you always know where you are </pre>
<pre>Stays accurate     Keeps counting even when the tab is in the background </pre>
<pre>Customizable       Set your own durations and volume in the settings </pre>
<pre>Responsive         Works on desktop, tablet, and mobile </pre>
&nbsp;

---

The Pomodoro Technique was developed by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s.<br>
The idea is simple: break your work into focused intervals, separated by short breaks. Your brain gets to rest before it needs it, which means you stay sharp for longer.<br>

&nbsp;
<pre>01   Pick one task you want to work on </pre>
<pre>02   Start the timer and work until it goes off ‚ÄĒ no switching, no checking </pre>
<pre>03   Take a short break. Step away. Do not think about the task </pre>
<pre>04   Repeat. After four rounds, take a longer break </pre>
&nbsp;

The default intervals are 25 minutes of work, 5 minutes of rest, and 15 minutes after every four sessions.<br>
These are a starting point, not a rule. Adjust them in the settings until they feel right for you.<br>
POMODORI tracks your four-session cycle with the dots at the bottom so you always know where you stand.
